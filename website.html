Filipino cuisine is a delightful fusion of flavors from its rich history

and diverse cultural influences. Spanning over 7,000 islands, the Philippines boasts a culinary

landscape as diverse as its landscape, where each region offers its unique dishes and cooking styles.

At the heart of Filipino cooking is the concept of "lutong bahay" or home-cooked meals,

emphasizing fresh ingredients, hearty portions, and communal dining. Staples like rice,

adobo (meat stewed in vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic), and sinigang (tangy tamarind soup)

form the backbone of Filipino meals, reflecting the country's agricultural abundance.
